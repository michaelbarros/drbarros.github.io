Short Bio

Dr Barros is an Assistant Professor (Lecturer) since June 2020 in the School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering at the University of Essex, UK. He received the PhD in Computer Science at the Waterford Institute of Technology in 2016. He previously held multiple academic positions as a Research Fellow in the Tampere University, Finland as well as the Waterford Institute of Technology, Ireland.

He has over 60 research peer-reviewed scientific publications in top journals and conferences such as IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, in the areas of molecular and unconventional communications, biomedical engineering, bionano science and 6G. Since 2020, he is a review editor for the Frontiers in Communications and Networks journal in the area of unconventional communications. He also served as guest editor for the IEEE Transactions on Molecular, Biological and Multi-Scale Communications and Digital Communications Networks journals.

He received the CONNECT Prof. Tom Brazil Excellence in Research Award in 2020. Dr Barros is awarded the European Commission's Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellowship (MSCA-IF) from 2019-2021, the Irish Research Council's (IRC) Government of Ireland Post-doc Fellow from 2016-2018 and the Enterprise Ireland's (EI) Commercialization Funding from 2018-2019, securing a total of €667k as a PI.

Research Interests

Molecular Communications

I seek to characterize and model how synthetic and natural biological cells or bionano machines communicate using the following media: calcium signalling, spikes, synapes and quorum sensing.
Internet of Bio-nano Things

I seek to design communication systems for connecting bionano devices, which are implanted to the human body or not, to the Internet.
Biocomputing

I seek to model and apply principles of synthetic biology and control theory to the design of biological bionano computing devices that can be used as fully biocompatible implantable sensors and actuators.
Molecular Communications and Computation on a Chip

I seek to design novel devices called Molecular Communications and Computation on a Chip where I use molecular communication system inside microfluidic devices to perform experiments with biological systems.
Computational Pathology

I seek to develop computational mathematical models that describe the signalling pathways changes at cellular level when biological tissues undergo diseases, including Alzeihmer's and Epilepsy.
Neuroscience

I seek to develop novel models for neuroscience using molecular communications, information theory and multi-scale modelling.
